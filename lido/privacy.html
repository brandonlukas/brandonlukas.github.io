Privacy

Lido collects nothing. The rest of this page is detail.

Effective July 10, 2026

Your data stays on your phone

Workouts, the pool's history, journal notes, and settings live on your device. There are no accounts, no sign-ins, and no Lido servers — nothing is uploaded anywhere. If your iPhone is backed up (iCloud or computer), Lido's data is included in that backup like any app's, under Apple's terms, not ours.

No tracking of any kind

Lido contains no analytics, no advertising, no crash reporters, and no third-party code that phones home. The app makes no network requests of its own.

Sharing a workout shares only that workout

When you share a workout, the program itself — exercise names, sets, durations — is encoded directly inside the link or file you send. It goes from your phone to whoever you send it to, and never passes through a server we run. Your history is stripped before it travels; only the program goes.

This website

These pages are static files hosted on GitHub Pages, with no analytics or cookies of ours. GitHub may log standard technical request data (like IP addresses) per the GitHub Privacy Statement. If a shared workout link opens here as a web page, the workout is decoded in your browser — the part of the link that carries it is never sent to any server.

Deleting your data

Delete a workout or logbook entry in the app to remove it. Delete the app to remove everything Lido ever stored.

Changes and contact

If any of this ever changes, this page changes with it, with a new date at the top. Questions: lidoworkoutapp@gmail.com.
